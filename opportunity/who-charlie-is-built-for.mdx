Charlie is purpose-built for one type of merchant: Shopify Plus brands running physical retail alongside their online channel.

The problem Charlie solves only exists when a brand has inventory in multiple locations it needs to orchestrate intelligently. Here is how to identify those accounts quickly.

## The ideal profile.

<CardGroup cols={2}>
  <Card title="Shopify Plus" icon="shopify">
    The merchant is on Shopify Plus and uses Shopify POS in at least 1 physical location.
  </Card>
  <Card title="2+ locations" icon="store">
    They operate stores, a warehouse, or both — and fulfill online orders from any of them.
  </Card>
  <Card title="€1M+ GMV online" icon="chart-bar">
    Enough online volume for fulfillment decisions to have real financial impact.
  </Card>
  <Card title="Omnichannel intent" icon="arrows-split-up-and-left">
    They're actively pushing ship-from-store, click and collect, or endless aisle — or want to.
  </Card>
</CardGroup>

## Signals that a prospect is ready.

| Signal | What it means |
|---|---|
| "Our stores are overwhelmed during peak" | Capacity management problem |
| "We're overselling online because stores don't update stock fast enough" | Safety stock problem |
| "Our store managers don't see the value they bring" | Revenue attribution problem |
| "We route manually or it's a black box" | Smart routing problem |
| "We looked at an OMS but it was too complex / too expensive" | Charlie's sweet spot |

## Who Charlie is not for.

Not every Shopify Plus merchant needs Charlie. Skip accounts where:

- They're pure e-commerce with no physical locations
- They have a single warehouse and no stores
- They're already live on a full OMS (Cegid, Manhattan, etc.) and satisfied
- They're below Shopify Plus or GMV is too small for ROI to be compelling
