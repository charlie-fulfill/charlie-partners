## Charlie in one sentence.

Charlie does what Shopify doesn't — OMS, omnichannel analytics, and in-store operations, all inside the platform your teams already use.

---

## Common objections.

### "Shopify already handles our fulfillment"

Shopify's order routing is basic and static — built for brands with 2 or 3 locations, not more. In practice, 90% of orders end up allocated to the same 10% of locations. That's not routing, that's a default.

Shopify gives merchants around 5 routing rules. Charlie gives them 50+, making it possible to distribute orders more evenly across locations and give each store real control over what it handles.

No safety stock buffers, no daily capacity limits, no revenue attribution per store, no omnichannel analytics. Most Shopify Plus brands are routing on autopilot and flying blind on store performance.

**Questions to ask:**
- *"How does Shopify decide which location fulfills an order right now?"*
- *"Are your stores complaining about the volume they receive?"*
- *"Do you see many cancellations or transfers after an order is assigned?"*

If stores are cancelling or transferring orders, it means they're not playing along — and there's significant manual rework happening behind the scenes.

---

### "We're evaluating a full OMS"

A classic OMS takes 6 to 18 months to go live, costs $100k+ in setup, and adds a second interface your client's teams have to use every day.

Charlie goes live in a few days, costs $350 to $3,000/month, and lives entirely inside Shopify. OMS capabilities, omnichannel analytics, and in-store operations — without replacing anything they already use.

Charlie is also a smart way to test before committing. Before investing months of time and significant budget in a full OMS implementation, brands can go live with Charlie quickly and validate exactly what they need — with real data, real usage, and no CAPEX.

**The question to ask:** *"What's the timeline and budget they're working with — and what are they trying to validate before committing?"*

---

### "It's not a priority right now"

The cost of not solving this is silent but real: stock misallocated across locations, stores overwhelmed during peak, revenue attributed to the wrong channel, store managers with no visibility into their real contribution.

Charlie goes live in a few days. The effort to evaluate it is minimal.

---

### "We don't want another tool to manage"

Charlie doesn't add a tool. It lives inside Shopify and Shopify POS — the same interface your client's teams already use. There is no second screen, no new login, no training.

---

### "How is this different from what Shopify does natively?"

| Capability | Shopify native | Charlie |
|---|---|---|
| Order routing | ~5 static rules | 50+ dynamic rules: capacity, stock, tags, product type |
| Safety stock | Not available | Per-location buffer stock |
| Capacity management | Not available | Daily order limits per location |
| Revenue attribution | Not available | Per-location tracking with custom rules |
| Omnichannel analytics | Not available | Cross-channel retail performance |

---

### "We have systems outside Shopify — marketplaces, B2B, a separate POS"

Charlie is Shopify-native. If a meaningful part of the merchant's commerce runs outside Shopify, Charlie isn't the right fit — and it's better to say so clearly than to force it.
